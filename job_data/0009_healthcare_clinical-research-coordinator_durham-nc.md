## About IQVIA

IQVIA is the world's leading contract research organization, combining advanced analytics, transformative technology, and extensive domain expertise to accelerate the development of innovative medical treatments. Partnering with top pharmaceutical and biotech sponsors worldwide, IQVIA orchestrates clinical trials at global scale, turning data into actionable insights that bring therapies to patients faster.

## About the Role

As a Clinical Research Coordinator at IQVIA's Durham office, you will be at the center of clinical trial operations, ensuring that studies are executed with precision, regulatory compliance, and a steadfast commitment to participant safety. You will work alongside investigators, sponsors, and cross-functional teams to manage trial activities from patient recruitment through data collection and regulatory submission. This hybrid position is ideal for individuals passionate about advancing drug development and building a career at the intersection of science, operations, and patient care.

## Responsibilities

- Coordinate and manage day-to-day clinical trial activities in accordance with study protocols, GCP/ICH guidelines, and applicable regulatory requirements
- Screen, recruit, and enroll study participants, conducting informed consent processes and ensuring eligibility criteria are met
- Collect, enter, and verify clinical data using electronic data capture (EDC) systems, maintaining accuracy, completeness, and audit readiness
- Prepare, organize, and maintain regulatory documentation including IRB submissions, protocol amendments, and essential regulatory binders
- Serve as the primary point of contact for study participants, clinical staff, sponsors, and monitors, facilitating clear communication and timely resolution of queries

## Requirements

- **Education:** Bachelor's degree in Life Sciences, Nursing, Public Health, or a related field
- **Experience:** 0-2 years of experience in clinical research, healthcare, or a related setting; internship or academic research experience is considered
- **Skills:** Clinical trial protocols, GCP/ICH guidelines, electronic data capture (EDC) systems, patient recruitment, regulatory documentation, and proficiency in Microsoft Office
- **Certifications:** ACRP (CCRC) or SOCRA (CCRP) certification preferred but not required
- **Additional:** Excellent organizational and communication skills, ability to manage multiple studies simultaneously, and a detail-oriented approach to data management and regulatory compliance

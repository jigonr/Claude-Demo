## About Mayo Clinic

Mayo Clinic is a nonprofit organization committed to clinical practice, education, and research, providing expert whole-person care to everyone who needs healing. Guided by the principle that "the needs of the patient come first," Mayo Clinic's integrated, multispecialty team model has set the standard for healthcare delivery worldwide.

## About the Role

As a Medical Laboratory Technician in the Department of Laboratory Medicine and Pathology, you will be an essential member of one of the largest and most respected clinical laboratories in the world. You will perform diagnostic testing across multiple laboratory disciplines, generating the accurate and timely results that Mayo Clinic physicians depend on to guide patient care. This role offers the opportunity to grow within a collaborative, world-class environment where laboratory professionals are valued as integral partners in the diagnostic process.

## Responsibilities

- Perform routine and specialized laboratory testing in hematology, clinical chemistry, urinalysis, and coagulation following established Mayo Clinic protocols
- Collect, process, and prepare patient specimens while maintaining strict chain-of-custody and specimen integrity standards
- Operate and maintain laboratory instrumentation, performing daily calibrations, quality control checks, and basic troubleshooting as required
- Accurately enter, verify, and release test results through the laboratory information system (LIS), ensuring timely reporting to clinical teams
- Adhere to all quality assurance and safety protocols, including participation in proficiency testing programs and compliance with CAP and CLIA regulations
- Collaborate with pathologists, medical technologists, and clinical staff to resolve discrepant results and support patient care across Mayo Clinic's integrated practice

## Requirements

- **Education:** Associate's degree in Medical Laboratory Technology or a related clinical laboratory science program
- **Experience:** 0-2 years of clinical laboratory experience; new graduates from accredited MLT programs are encouraged to apply
- **Skills:** Specimen processing, hematology, clinical chemistry, laboratory information systems (LIS), quality control, and phlebotomy
- **Certifications:** ASCP Medical Laboratory Technician (MLT) certification required, or eligibility to obtain within 12 months of hire
- **Additional:** Strong attention to detail, ability to work in a fast-paced environment, and commitment to Mayo Clinic's values of respect, integrity, compassion, healing, teamwork, and excellence

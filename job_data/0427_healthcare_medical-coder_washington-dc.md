## About Mayo Clinic

Mayo Clinic is the nation's first and largest integrated not-for-profit medical group practice. Our commitment to patients, research, and education guides everything we do—from groundbreaking treatments to training the next generation of healthcare professionals. Our Washington, DC location serves as a vital hub for patient care and clinical education.

## About the Role

As a Medical Coder at Mayo Clinic, you'll play a critical role in transforming clinical documentation into accurate, compliant coded data. Your precision ensures proper billing, supports quality research, and contributes to the comprehensive health records that guide patient care. Work in a supportive hybrid environment where your accuracy is valued and your growth is supported.

## Responsibilities

- Review patient medical records and clinical documentation to accurately assign ICD-10, CPT, and HCPCS codes
- Verify coding accuracy and compliance with coding guidelines and regulatory standards
- Communicate with physicians and clinical staff regarding documentation deficiencies or clarifications needed
- Maintain current knowledge of coding standards, payer guidelines, and regulatory changes
- Audit coded records for accuracy and completeness, documenting findings and recommendations
- Achieve departmental targets for productivity while maintaining accuracy standards of 98% or higher

## Requirements

**Education**
Associate's degree in Health Information Management or Medical Coding; or High School diploma with completion of accredited medical coding program

**Experience**
1–3 years in medical coding or health information management

**Skills**
- Proficiency with ICD-10, CPT, and HCPCS coding systems
- Knowledge of medical terminology and anatomy
- Attention to detail and accuracy
- Familiarity with EHR systems (Epic preferred)
- Strong organizational skills
- Ability to work independently and meet deadlines

**Certifications**
- RHIT (Registered Health Information Technician) or AAPC CPC (Certified Professional Coder) preferred
- AHIMA or AAPC membership required within 6 months of hire

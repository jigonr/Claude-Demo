# Phlebotomy Technician

**CDC** | Government/Public Health | Remote | $30,000–$39,000/year | Full-time | Posted Jan 18, 2026

## About CDC

The Centers for Disease Control and Prevention is the leading federal agency for public health, protecting Americans from disease and bioterror threats. Working at the CDC means contributing to life-saving research and surveillance that impacts millions of people globally.

## About the Role

As a Phlebotomy Technician at CDC, you'll be responsible for collecting and processing biological specimens that feed into critical epidemiological and diagnostic research. Your precision and adherence to safety protocols directly enable the public health discoveries that protect communities. This position offers meaningful work in service of national health initiatives, with the support and resources of a premier federal agency.

## Responsibilities

- Perform venipuncture and other specimen collection procedures in accordance with CDC and CLIA standards, maintaining meticulous record-keeping
- Process biological specimens for laboratory analysis, ensuring proper handling, labeling, and storage protocols
- Maintain sterile technique and adherence to infection control procedures to safeguard patient and staff safety
- Communicate effectively with patients and research participants, explaining procedures and addressing concerns with professionalism
- Monitor inventory of phlebotomy supplies and equipment; report equipment malfunctions to supervisory staff

## Requirements

**Education**
High school diploma or GED; completion of accredited phlebotomy program

**Experience**
0-2 years of experience (entry-level applicants encouraged to apply)

**Skills**
- Venipuncture technique
- Specimen handling and processing
- CLIA and OSHA compliance knowledge
- Electronic medical records systems
- Patient communication

**Certifications**
Phlebotomy Technician Certification (ASCP or equivalent)

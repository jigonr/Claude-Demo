# Health Information Technician

**Company:** Fortrea | **Category:** Healthcare | **Location:** Milwaukee, WI | **Salary:** $32,000–$42,000 | **Type:** Full-Time | **Posted:** 2026-03-21

## About Fortrea

Fortrea is a leading clinical research organization providing a comprehensive suite of services to the global biopharmaceutical and medical device industries. We support Phase I through Phase IV clinical trials and have operations across North America, Europe, and Asia.

## About the Role

The Health Information Technician plays a critical role in clinical research operations, ensuring the accuracy, security, and accessibility of patient data that forms the foundation of our studies. This position offers the opportunity to contribute directly to the advancement of medical science while developing valuable expertise in healthcare information management.

## Responsibilities

- Manage and maintain patient medical records in accordance with HIPAA and institutional standards
- Enter and verify patient data into electronic health record systems with high accuracy
- Abstract clinical information from source documents to support research protocols
- File, retrieve, and organize medical documentation according to established systems
- Assist with audit preparation and regulatory compliance documentation

## Requirements

**Education:** High school diploma or GED; certification in Health Information Technology preferred

**Experience:** 1-2 years of medical records or health information management experience

**Skills:**
- Proficiency with electronic health record systems
- Strong organizational and filing abilities
- Excellent attention to detail and accuracy
- HIPAA compliance understanding
- Basic computer skills and data entry competency

**Certifications:** Registered Health Information Technician (RHIT) preferred

- Health, dental, and vision insurance
- 401(k) plan with company match
- Paid time off and paid holidays
- Professional development and certification support
- Opportunity to contribute to advancing medical research

## About Pfizer

Pfizer is a global pharmaceutical and biotechnology corporation dedicated to developing breakthroughs that change patients' lives. With a robust pipeline spanning oncology, immunology, rare disease, and infectious disease, Pfizer combines cutting-edge science with operational excellence to deliver transformative medicines and vaccines to people around the world.

## About the Role

As a Research Associate I at Pfizer's Cambridge research hub, you will play a hands-on role in early-stage drug discovery, executing experiments that generate the critical data scientists need to advance therapeutic candidates through the pipeline. You will work within a collaborative, multidisciplinary team environment where scientific rigor and innovation are paramount. This position offers a strong foundation for building a career in pharmaceutical research, with exposure to diverse therapeutic areas and state-of-the-art laboratory technologies.

## Responsibilities

- Execute cell-based assays including cell culture maintenance, passage, and experimental setup in support of drug discovery and target validation programs
- Perform molecular and immunological techniques including PCR, ELISA, and flow cytometry according to established protocols and GLP compliance standards
- Collect, organize, and analyze experimental data using GraphPad Prism, R, or equivalent statistical tools, and present findings to project teams
- Maintain accurate and detailed electronic laboratory notebooks and documentation in compliance with Pfizer's research data integrity standards
- Contribute to the optimization and troubleshooting of experimental protocols, identifying opportunities to improve assay sensitivity, reproducibility, and throughput
- Collaborate with scientists, principal investigators, and cross-functional teams to support pipeline advancement from target identification through lead optimization

## Requirements

- **Education:** Bachelor's degree in Biology, Biochemistry, Molecular Biology, or a related life sciences discipline
- **Experience:** 0-2 years of laboratory research experience; academic research or internship experience in a relevant field is valued
- **Skills:** Cell culture, PCR, ELISA, flow cytometry, data analysis (GraphPad Prism and/or R), and familiarity with GLP compliance standards
- **Certifications:** None required
- **Additional:** Strong scientific curiosity, meticulous attention to experimental detail, and the ability to work effectively in a team-oriented, fast-paced research environment
